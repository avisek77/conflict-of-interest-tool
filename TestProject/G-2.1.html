Section G-2.1: Identifying Conflicts of Interest

Key Definitions

Before proceeding with the conflict identification questions, please review these important definitions that will guide your assessment:

Interest

An interest is a commitment or concern that you have. It can be something that you care about, are responsible for, or that affects you in some way. Interests can be financial, professional, personal, or relational in nature.

Competing Interest

A competing interest exists when you have two or more interests that could potentially conflict with each other or require different courses of action. These interests may pull you in different directions or create tension in your decision-making.

Conflict of Interest

A conflict of interest exists when your ability to fulfill one commitment or responsibility could be compromised by your other interests, relationships, or obligations. This occurs when competing interests could reasonably be perceived to influence your professional judgment or decision-making.

Self-oriented interests include (but are not limited to):
  • Financial interests: salary, fees, bonuses, investments, shareholdings, business interests
  • Career advancement: promotion opportunities, professional development, skill building
  • Professional reputation: recognition, awards, publications, speaking opportunities
  • Personal relationships: networking opportunities, social connections that could benefit you
  • Access and resources: information, facilities, equipment, databases, networks
  • Personal beliefs and values: ideological commitments, political views, religious beliefs
  • Family interests: spouse's career, children's education, family financial security
  • Personal convenience: location preferences, schedule flexibility, work-life balance
Be specific about how these interests could be positively or negatively affected by the new activity. Consider both direct and indirect impacts.
Other-oriented interests include (but are not limited to):
  • Employer commitments: duties to your current employer, contractual obligations, loyalty requirements
  • Client/patient obligations: duties of care, confidentiality, best interests of those you serve
  • Student/research responsibilities: educational duties, research integrity, participant welfare
  • Professional body duties: obligations to professional associations, regulatory bodies, licensing authorities
  • Community commitments: responsibilities to community groups, charitable organizations, volunteer roles
  • Family obligations: duties to dependents, caregiving responsibilities, family business interests
  • Collaborative commitments: obligations arising from partnerships, joint ventures, team responsibilities
  • Public interest duties: responsibilities to the broader public or specific communities
Be specific about your commitments to others and how the new activity might impact your ability to fulfill these obligations.
