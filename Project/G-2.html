CONTEGRITY

Section G-2: Current Circumstances

Understanding Your Current Position

To properly assess potential conflicts of interest, we need to understand your current professional situation, roles, commitments, and interests. This comprehensive information helps identify where conflicts might arise and ensures nothing important is overlooked.

Please be thorough in your responses as incomplete information may result in an inadequate assessment of potential conflicts.

Provide comprehensive details about:
  • Your official job title and position level
  • Key responsibilities and areas of authority
  • Decision-making powers and approval authorities
  • Fiduciary duties or stewardship responsibilities
  • Supervisory or management responsibilities
  • Budget or resource management responsibilities
  • External representation or spokesperson duties
Include information about:
  • Full name and type of your primary employer or organisation
  • Sector (government, private, non-profit, academic, healthcare, etc.)
  • Size and scope of the organization
  • Your department, division, or unit within the organization
  • Any parent companies, subsidiaries, or affiliated organizations
  • Geographic scope of operations (local, national, international)
List all other professional activities including:
  • Secondary employment or part-time roles
  • Board positions, directorships, or trustee roles
  • Consulting, advisory, or freelance work
  • Research collaborations or academic appointments
  • Professional association roles or committee memberships
  • Volunteer positions with professional relevance
  • Speaking engagements, writing, or media activities
  • Investment activities or business ownership
Include any financial interests such as:
  • Direct shareholdings in companies or organizations
  • Investment portfolios, mutual funds, or managed funds
  • Business ownership, partnerships, or joint ventures
  • Intellectual property rights or royalty arrangements
  • Real estate investments or property development interests
  • Family financial interests that could influence your decisions
  • Debt relationships or financial obligations to relevant parties
  • Trust arrangements or beneficial interests
Consider relationships such as:
  • Family members employed by relevant organizations
  • Close personal friends in positions of influence
  • Romantic relationships with professional implications
  • Business partnerships with personal dimensions
  • Mentoring relationships (as mentor or mentee)
  • Social or community connections with professional overlap
  • Historical relationships that could create perceptions of bias
